# Philosophy

Chronicle begins from durability rather than distribution.

Most digital products are built around platforms, identities, or applications. Chronicle asks what remains when those systems decay.

A durable digital object should:

- survive the original application
- survive the original company
- survive the original runtime
- remain interpretable by third parties
- remain verifiable without trusting the original narrator

Receipts have these properties more naturally than most digital artifacts.

A receipt is evidence-bearing. It can be replayed, checked, challenged, and referenced. It anchors claims to observable history rather than to branding alone.

That makes receipts a plausible candidate for a new class of digital object:

- not just media
- not just identifiers
- not just ownership pointers
- but durable verifiable history

Chronicle therefore treats history as the scarce thing.
Not synthetic scarcity. Not social hype. Not platform enclosure.

The claim is not that every receipt becomes valuable.
The claim is that verified history may become one of the most important substrates of digital value.
