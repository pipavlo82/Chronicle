# Ownership

## What makes a receipt ownable?

A receipt may be ownable when there is a meaningful claim over:

- authorship
- stewardship
- legal control
- economic rights
- archival control
- transfer rights

Chronicle does not assume that ownership is always singular.
A receipt may support:

- creator ownership
- organizational ownership
- custodian stewardship
- split economic rights
- public-verification without exclusive ownership

## Ownership versus truth

Ownership does not create truth.

A receipt remains valuable only if verification remains independent of the owner.
That means Chronicle must keep a strict separation between:

- who controls or holds the receipt
- what the receipt proves

## Long-lived identity

If receipts are durable, ownership should ideally remain portable across:

- platforms
- companies
- identity systems
- runtimes
- model generations

Chronicle therefore prefers ownership models that do not require permanent dependence on the original producer.
